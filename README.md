## 📌 Project Description

This project presents a detailed **Empathy Map** created as part of UX research for a **Fitness Mobile Application**. The goal was to understand the target user's thoughts, feelings, behaviors, and frustrations to design a more user-centric fitness experience.

---

## 👤 User Persona

- **Name:** Rohit Saharan
- **Age:** 27 years old
- **Occupation:** Software Engineer
- **Goal:** Stay fit and healthy

---

## 🗺️ Empathy Map Overview

The empathy map is divided into 4 key quadrants:

| Quadrant | Insight |
|----------|---------|
| **Think** | Wants to stay fit but struggles with consistency |
| **Feel** | Feels guilty about missing workouts; stressed about health |
| **Say** | Talks about fitness goals but finds it hard to follow through |
| **Do** | Uses fitness apps occasionally; skips gym due to busy schedule |

---

## 🔑 Key User Insights

- ⏰ **Time** is the biggest barrier — users want quick and flexible workout options
- 😟 Users feel **guilty and stressed** about not being consistent
- 📱 **Mobile-first experience** is essential — they prefer apps over physical interactions
- 💡 **Motivation** is low and inconsistent — they need reminders, tracking, and encouragement
- ❤️ Health is a priority, but **convenience drives decisions**

---

## 🎯 Key Takeaway

> Busy office professionals want a fitness app that fits into their hectic schedules, offers quick routines, keeps them motivated, and helps them build consistent healthy habits with ease.
